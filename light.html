THEFLASH

Season 3

Premieres Tuesday October 4 at 8pm on The CW

“WHAT WOULD YOU SACRIFICE TO HAVE EVERYTHING YOU’VE EVER WANTED? — Barry (Grant Gustin) is living his dream life – his parents are alive, he’s asked Iris West (Candice Patton) on a date and he can finally be a normal guy as Central City has another speedster, Kid Flash (Keiynan Lonsdale), running around saving the city. When Barry starts forgetting parts of his old life, the Reverse Flash (guest star Matt Letscher) taunts his nemesis and tells him that there will be serious repercussions for Barry and the ones he loves if he continues to live in this alternate universe. In addition to losing his memories, his powers will also start to fade. When disaster strikes, Barry must decide if he wants to continue to live in this world as Barry Allen or return to his universe as The Flash.”

While the synopsis doesn’t reveal details that could not have been pieced together from the various promos released thus far, it is exciting nonetheless to see everything spelled out. Much like the “Flashpoint” comic, Barry will have to struggle with losing his memories of the original timeline, and he will start the season with his mother alive. Unlike the comics, however, Barry will retain his super-speed, but from the sound of things, his abilities will fade the longer he stays in the “Flashpoint” timeline.

Perhaps the most interesting element in the synopsis, is the reference to “disaster” striking. We know that Barry will be dealing with two major villains this season — Savitar and Dr. Alchemy. The question is which universe do they show up in first, and could we be seeing the effects of their villainy as early as episode 1? We’ll find out soon enough. It looks like season 3 of The Flash is going to be the biggest yet, and even after Team Flash makes it through “Flashpoint,” things might not simply go back to normal.
